# Mastery Architecture

## Purpose

Mastery represents the system's current interpretation of learner capability.

Mastery is not a record of individual performances.

Mastery is an accumulated interpretation derived from many evaluations over time.

Mastery answers:

"What can this learner reliably demonstrate today?"

---

## Mastery Ownership

Mastery belongs to the Learner.

Evaluations contribute to Mastery.

Mastery does not belong to any individual Attempt, Evidence record, or Evaluation.

Mastery emerges from the accumulation of judgments.

---

## Evaluations vs Mastery

Evaluations preserve historical judgments.

Mastery preserves current interpretation.

Evaluations answer:

"What did we conclude at the time?"

Mastery answers:

"What do we currently believe?"

Evaluations are historical.

Mastery is living.

---

## Capability Ledger

The Capability Ledger preserves every historical Evaluation.

Nothing is discarded.

The ledger acts as the permanent record of capability development.

Mastery derives its interpretation from the ledger.

Mastery should never replace historical evidence.

---

## Mastery Dimensions

Mastery is decomposed into capability dimensions.

Current dimensions include:

- Reconstruction
- Transfer
- Reasoning
- Independence
- Failure Awareness

Dimensions may evolve through governance processes.

New dimensions should be added cautiously.

---

## Mastery Accumulation

Mastery is derived.

It is never manually assigned.

New Evaluations contribute additional information to the learner's Capability Ledger.

Mastery continuously reinterprets the accumulated body of evidence.

Mastery should become more accurate as evidence grows.

---

## Mastery Snapshots

Mastery snapshots preserve historical growth.

A snapshot records the system's interpretation at a particular point in time.

Snapshots enable:

- Progress tracking
- Historical comparison
- Capability trajectory analysis
- Auditability

Snapshots preserve growth without losing historical context.

---

## Mastery Relationships

```text
Evidence
  ↓
Evaluation
  ↓
Capability Ledger
  ↓
Mastery
  ↓
Standing
```

Evidence creates observations.

Evaluations create judgments.

The Capability Ledger preserves judgments.

Mastery interprets accumulated judgments.

Standing contextualizes Mastery.

---

## Mastery Principles

- Mastery is derived.
- Mastery is living.
- Evaluations preserve judgment.
- Capability Ledgers preserve history.
- Mastery preserves current interpretation.
- Growth should remain visible.
- Historical truth should remain auditable.
- Capability trajectory matters.
